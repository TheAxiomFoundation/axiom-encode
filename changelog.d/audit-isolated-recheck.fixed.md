The waiver audit re-executes any expectation-violating module in isolation before reporting: concurrent fan-out occasionally perturbs an engine subprocess's outcome under load (observed ~1 per 3,099 modules per run), and a transient flake must not become a waiver accusation. The module's standalone value is the partition-invariant truth; real drift and stale waivers survive isolation, load flakes do not. Rechecked rows carry `isolated_recheck: true` in the report.
